The London Tech Scene

May 20th, 2014

London is a fantastic place, but there is so much going on that sometimes is fantastically tough to get your bearings.

In the short time that I have been here I have gone to some excellent events and met many incredible people.

Here are a few recommendations for everyone that decided to give the city a go:

Find a job:

  • 3beards: a job board created by London legends
  • Work in Startups: lists all kinds of startup jobs
  • Campus London: jobs announced by Campus London (a space set up by Google). Make sure to check the job board on the walls of the cafe too.
  • Mind the Product: jobs for Product Managers
  • StackOverflow Careers: mostly jobs for developers.
  • Silicon Milk Roundabout: hands down the best job fair ever

Events:

  • Hacks/Hackers: The magic intersection between journalists & developers.
  • Hacker News London: Meet fellow HN addicts & listen to hackers talk.
  • Minibar: Monthly tech meetup in a great space. Focus changes but quality stays high.
  • Campus London events: Everything happening at Campus. It is a busy place.
  • TechHub events: The most famous co-working space in the city. Most events are open to the public.

Understand the tech scene:

  • roundabout.io: a map of digital London (startups, agencies, digital companies, etc.)

Meet people:

  • Silicon Drinkabout: Laid back after-work event on Fridays.

Newsletters:

  • Fetch: a weekly blast of all the best tech events in the city.
  • The ‘Stache by 3beards: a weekly update by the beards.

Awesome London apps:

  • Citymapper: get where you need to go. Really well executed app. Don’t leave home without it.
  • YPlan: find out what is happening in the city and instantly book events.
  • Uber: not an app from London, but a great app for getting around London.

Also, Rosario Rascuna (@_sarhus) wrote an excellent post on living in London, it should really be required reading: “A short guide for all the hackers willing to live in London”
